How to Play Tic-Tac-Toe

Welcome to the classic game of Tic-Tac-Toe! Can you outwit your opponent and claim victory in this age-old battle of Xs and Os? Here's how it works:

Game Setup:

Tic-Tac-Toe is played on a 3x3 grid. Two players take turns to place their symbol (X or O) on the grid until one of them wins or the game ends in a draw.

Gameplay:

Players take turns to make a move. Each move consists of placing your symbol (X or O) in an empty square on the grid. The objective is to form a line of three of your symbols either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

Game Rules:

Here are the basic rules to keep in mind:

  • A player wins if they are the first to create a line of three of their symbols in a row, column, or diagonal.
  • If all the squares on the grid are filled, and no player has won, the game is a draw.
  • Players take turns, and each player can only place their own symbol on an empty square.

Scoring:

Scoring is simple: you win the game by getting three of your symbols in a row. There are no points for style or flair; it's all about strategy and placement.

Game Etiquette:

Remember to be a good sport. Congratulate your opponent on a well-played game, and don't gloat if you win. It's all in the spirit of fun and competition!
